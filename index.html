How to read the results

The capital shown is what you need to invest today — not in the future. The time horizon models how dividends evolve over the years, compounding uncertainty. A longer horizon produces a wider spread between pessimistic and optimistic scenarios. Always use the Base (median) figure as your planning target, with a safety margin toward the pessimistic scenario.

Parameter glossary

  • Dividend Yield — Annual income as % of portfolio value. Example: 4% on €100k = €4,000/year gross.
  • Annual Growth — Expected yearly increase in dividen. Historically ~3–8% for quality companies.
  • Tax Rate — Tax applied to dividends (USA: 15%, UK: 8.75%, IT: 26%).
  • Growth Volatility — Year-to-year unpredictability. Higher = more uncertainty.
  • Time Horizon — Number of years simulated, not when you need the money.
  • Simulations — Number of Monte Carlo runs. More = more reliable results.

What this tool does

This simulator uses a Monte Carlo model to estimate the capital required to generate a target monthly income from dividends. It simulates thousands of possible future scenarios by introducing randomness in dividend growth.

Each simulation adjusts dividend yield over time using a stochastic process (normal distribution), then calculates the capital required to sustain the desired income after taxes.

The output shows a distribution of outcomes (pessimistic, median, optimistic), helping you understand risk and uncertainty instead of relying on a single deterministic estimate.
